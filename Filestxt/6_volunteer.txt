About the program

The Sustainability Volunteer Program, run through the Office of Sustainability as a complement to the Sustainability Ambassadors Program, is your one-stop-shop for sustainability volunteering!
There are many initiatives in our sustainability ecosystem on campus, from student groups to university offices to academic departments!
Our Sustainability Volunteering Program, currently in its pilot phase, aims to connect you with these amazing volunteer opportunities with ease! We use a program called Cervis to manage volunteer registration, placement, and shifts.
Benefits of participating include: 

A targeted quick to support you in identifying your best fit for volunteering

Recognition on co-curricular record for your volunteer horus

Virtual certificate recognizing time spent volunteering (for 25+ hours)

Volunteer appreciation events

Exclusive and early-bird offers to sustainability events and opportunities 











Program requirements


All students who register for the program must complete the one-hour Moodle sustainability training before they can begin volunteering with campaigns
Some individual campaigns will have additional training that is required in order to volunteer with them-- it depends on the group!
There is no time committment required to join-- once you're signed up, pick and choose the volunteer slots and opportunities that work best for you!











How to get started

Register for the program through Cervis here

Take the one-hour Moodle sustainability training

Complete the skills and interests form

Start signing up for shifts!
